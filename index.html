Aline Bezdikian

Aline Bezdikian-Shirajian is an Armenian-American educator, children's author, and cultural advocate who has dedicated her life to preserving and promoting the Armenian language, history, and culture.

Born in Lebanon and currently residing in Glendale, California, Aline has inspired new generations throughout her professional career to strengthen their connection to their Armenian identity through language, literature, and creative education. For many years, she has been teaching Armenian language, Armenian history, theater, and journalism at the high school level, helping countless students develop both their knowledge and their sense of national and cultural identity.

Aline is the author and creator of the "Lori and Nareg" children's book series. She has published 17 books in this collection, featuring a sister and a brother, Lori and Nareg. Through her stories, she introduces children to Armenian traditions, culture, geography, and history while also conveying important moral values.

In addition to her literary work, Aline has created Armenian educational games, including "Armenia's Mountains and Rivers", "Gata!" and "Tickets For Armenia", which are designed to teach Armenia's geography and culture to children and families in an enjoyable and engaging way.

Aline has also worked as a freelance journalist and columnist for various Armenian newspapers and magazines in both the United States and Lebanon. As a broadcaster and host, she has helped bring Armenian stories, personalities, and experiences to a wider audience. She has hosted a televised evening storytelling program for children and has been actively involved in the performing arts and theater.

Aline Bezdikian-Shirajian is married and lives in Glendale, California. She is the proud mother of a daughter and a son, Lori and Nareg, who inspired her to create the names of the main characters in her children's book series.
